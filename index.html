The moment I called it what it was

Taking money for knowledge people cannot use is not coaching.
I called it what it was: bad karma. And I stopped.

For ten years I filled rooms. 13,000 people trained. Every seminar was a success by every metric that the training industry uses to measure success. And then one day someone said something that I could not unhear: "I know where I am stuck. And I am still stuck." He had paid me. He had attended. He had taken notes. And nothing had changed.

I heard it again. And again. I started asking myself a different question — not "did I deliver the session?" but "did I do my job?" The answer, honestly, was no. I had delivered information. I had created inspiration. I had given people the feeling that change was possible. And then I had left — and the Monday arrived exactly as it had always arrived.

"What if instead of telling him what to do — I stayed until it was done?"

That question became the foundation of everything Altus Corp does today. Not a programme. Not a seminar. Not a framework handed across a table. But a team that enters your business — and refuses to leave until the systems are live, the habits are formed, and the results are real.

I made one rule — one I have kept for 21 years: Maximum 20 founders at a time. No exceptions. Ever. Because depth is not possible at scale. And depth is the only thing that actually changes businesses.

About his work and approach
How is this different from a business coach or consultant?
A coach asks questions and helps you find answers. A consultant hands over a report and leaves. Neither builds anything inside your business. Manan assigns a dedicated Implementer who comes into your business every week, builds the systems, enforces the discipline and does not leave until the transformation is real. The distinction is not a matter of degree — it is a matter of kind.
What does "he stays until it is done" actually mean?
It means the engagement does not end at a calendar date. You may repeat the programme at no additional cost until you give written confirmation that you have recovered your investment. The only condition — no break between batches. Continuity of habit formation is non-negotiable. This is not a marketing claim. It is a commitment built into every engagement. He has operated this way for 21 years.
Why does Manan never work with more than 20 founders at a time?
Customised implementation is structurally impossible in a large group. It cannot happen when an implementer does not know your name, your team, your numbers or your specific structural problems. Depth requires proximity. This rule has not changed in 21 years. It will not change.
